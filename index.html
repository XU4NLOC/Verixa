What is a trade receivable?
When an exporter ships goods, the buyer owes payment on a future date. VIFC purchases this future payment at a discount, then offers you the return. You receive the face value when the buyer pays — it's not a loan, it's an ownership stake in a verified payment obligation.

Platform Guide
Learn how VIFC Trade Finance works
Reference
Platform Overview
VIFC (Vietnam International Finance Company) Trade Finance Platform connects institutional and retail investors with verified trade receivable assets from Vietnamese SMEs exporting to global buyers.
How it works: When an SME exports goods, they issue an invoice to the buyer. Instead of waiting 30-90 days for payment, the SME can monetize this receivable through VIFC. Investors purchase these receivables at a discount and receive the full face value when the buyer pays.

How to Invest
1
Complete Investor KYC
Verify your identity to comply with VIFC regulations. This takes ~2 minutes and requires a government-issued ID.
2
Deposit Funds
Add USDC to your VIFC wallet via crypto transfer or wire. Funds are held in segregated accounts via licensed banking partners.
3
Browse Verified Assets
Explore available trade receivables. Each asset shows buyer credit rating, shipment status, and expected return.
4
Invest & Mint Tokens
Select your investment amount. Tokens (ERC-1400) are minted representing your ownership of the receivable.
5
Receive Returns
When the buyer pays at maturity, your principal + interest is distributed to your wallet automatically via smart contract.
How It Works (By Stakeholder)
For SMEs (Exporters)
1. Upload Invoice: Submit your export invoice and supporting documents (PO, shipping docs).
2. Verification: VIFC verifies the invoice through buyer confirmation, logistics tracking, and customs data.
3. Receive Advance: Get immediate cash (up to 96% of invoice value) instead of waiting 30-90 days.
4. Repay at Maturity: The buyer pays the full invoice amount to SPV escrow; your obligation is satisfied.
For Investors
1. Browse Assets: Review verified receivables with buyer ratings, shipment status, and pricing.
2. Purchase at Discount: Buy tokens representing ownership of the receivable at a discount (e.g., $96 for $100 face value).
3. Hold to Maturity: Wait for buyer payment (typically 30-90 days). Track progress via dashboard.
4. Receive Face Value: Smart contract automatically distributes full face value to your wallet.
For Verification Partners
VIFC integrates with multiple data providers to verify each asset:
• Logistics: DHL, FedEx, Maersk — shipment tracking & delivery confirmation
• Customs: Vietnam Customs — export declaration validation
• Credit Bureau: Dun & Bradstreet — buyer credit rating & payment history
• Insurance: Euler Hermes — trade credit insurance coverage
Key Terms & Credit Ratings
Understanding credit ratings is essential for evaluating investment risk:
AAA Highest credit quality. Extremely low probability of default. Examples: Walmart, Apple, Samsung. Default probability: <0.1%
AA Very high credit quality. Very low default probability. Examples: Target, IKEA, H&M. Default probability: 0.1-0.3%
A High credit quality. Low default probability. Examples: Carrefour, Zara. Default probability: 0.3-0.5%
BBB Medium credit quality. Moderate default probability. May include smaller buyers. Default probability: 0.5-1%
BB Speculative. Higher default probability than investment grade. Default probability: 1-3%
Other Key Terms
Face Value The full amount the buyer will pay at maturity (e.g., $100)
Purchase Price The price you pay now to buy the receivable (e.g., $96)
Discount The difference between face and purchase price (e.g., 4% = $4)
Tenor Time until buyer payment is due (30, 60, 90 days, etc.)
APY Annual Percentage Yield — your annualized return (e.g., 17% for 4% over 90 days)
SPV Special Purpose Vehicle — legal entity that holds the receivable for investors
ERC-1400 Token standard for security tokens representing ownership of real-world assets
